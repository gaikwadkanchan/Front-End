HERBICIDES



Herbicides are used in agricultural and wildland ecosystems to reduce the density of weeds and promote the growth of desirable species. Use of herbicides in agroecosystems may change composition of weed populations. In wildlands, herbicides may increase the diversity of native species. Threats to plant biodiversity caused by habitat loss and invasive species are far greater than threats by use of herbicides. This article reviews concepts of weeds; principles of weed management; and categories, action, and fate of herbicides.
